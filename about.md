Hi! I am a Ph.D. student at Stanford Physics. My name is pronounced *jee-won*.

I'm working on mining and modeling large volumes of astrophysical data to better understand the evolution of our Universe. My research focuses on inferring the Hubble constant from a phenomenon called strong gravitational lensing.

I develop probabilistic machine learning methods that can be applied under a principled Bayesian framework. Broadly, I'm interested in Bayesian hierarchical models, variational inference, Bayesian neural networks, and uncertainty quantification. My thesis projects are high-dimensional regression tasks with a range of input data formats, including images, catalogs (tables), and time series.
